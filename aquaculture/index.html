AquaCulture Farming techniques

Pond Culture

This is one of the oldest and simplest forms of aquaculture. Ponds are constructed or modified to provide a suitable habitat for fish or shrimp. They can be freshwater or saltwater systems and are commonly used for species like tilapia, catfish, and shrimp. Pond culture often involves managing water quality, stocking density, and feeding.

Cage Culture

In this technique, cages made of netting or mesh are placed in natural bodies of water such as lakes, rivers, or coastal areas. Fish are stocked inside the cages, which allow water exchange while preventing fish from escaping and protecting them from predators. Cage culture is commonly used for species like salmon, trout, and sea bass.
